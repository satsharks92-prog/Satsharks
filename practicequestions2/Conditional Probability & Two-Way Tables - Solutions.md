## 📊 Topic Summary — Conditional Probability & Two-Way Tables

**Core Concepts Tested:**
1. Reading two-way frequency tables (cells, row/column totals, grand total)
2. Joint probability: P(A and B) = cell / grand total
3. Marginal probability: P(A) = row or column total / grand total
4. Conditional probability: P(A | B) = cell / total of condition B
5. Determining association by comparing conditional distributions
6. Complement rule: P(not A | B) = 1 − P(A | B)
7. Independence: P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B)
8. Set operations: "A but not B" = A − overlap

**Most Common Traps:**
- Using grand total as denominator for conditional probability (joint ≠ conditional)
- Reversing the conditional: P(A | B) ≠ P(B | A)
- Confusing raw counts with proportions when assessing association
- Forgetting to subtract overlap for "A but not B" problems
- Using addition instead of multiplication for the independence test
