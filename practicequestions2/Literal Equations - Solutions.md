## Question 1 🟢
**Correct Answer: A) y = 21 − 3x**

**Step-by-step:**
3x + y = 21. Subtract 3x from both sides: y = 21 − 3x.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- B) y = 3x − 21 — subtracted in the wrong order.
- C) y = 21 + 3x — added 3x instead of subtracting.
- D) This expression still contains y on the right side.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: B flips the sign. When moving 3x to the other side, it becomes −3x, not +3x subtracted from 21 in reverse.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: To isolate y, move everything else to the other side with the opposite operation.

⚡ FAST METHOD: "y = everything else on the other side with flipped signs." y = 21 − 3x.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — one-step rearrangement.

## Question 2 🟢
**Correct Answer: C) l = A/w**

**Step-by-step:**
A = lw. Divide both sides by w: l = A/w.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- A) Subtraction doesn't undo multiplication.
- B) Multiplied instead of divided.
- D) Divided in the wrong order — w/A ≠ A/w.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: D reverses numerator and denominator. "Solve for l" means l goes on top.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: If y = ab, then a = y/b. The variable you solve for goes in the numerator.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Cover l → what's left is A/w.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — instant formula rearrangement.

## Question 3 🟡
**Correct Answer: A) m = (10 + 2n)/5**

**Step-by-step:**
5m − 2n = 10. Add 2n: 5m = 10 + 2n. Divide by 5: m = (10 + 2n)/5.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- B) (10 − 2n)/5 — subtracted 2n instead of adding.
- C) 2 + 2n — divided only the 10 by 5, forgot to divide 2n by 5.
- D) 2 − 2n/5 — subtracted instead of added.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: B forgets to flip the sign of −2n when moving it. Moving −2n to the right makes it +2n.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Moving a term across the equals sign changes its sign.

⚡ FAST METHOD: 5m = 10 + 2n → m = (10 + 2n)/5.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — two steps.

## Question 4 🟡
**Correct Answer: B) a = 3b/(bc − 4)**

**Step-by-step:**
3/a + 4/b = c.
Isolate 3/a: 3/a = c − 4/b.
Find common denominator on right: 3/a = (bc − 4)/b.
Take reciprocal and multiply by 3: a = 3b/(bc − 4).

**Why other options are wrong:**
- A) a = 3/(c − 4/b) — this is equivalent to B when simplified, but B is the fully simplified form. On the DSAT, the simplified version is the expected answer.
- C) Inverted — this is 1/a, not a.
- D) Added 4/b instead of subtracting.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: When the variable is in a denominator, solving requires taking a reciprocal. Students often forget this step and get 1/a instead of a.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: If k/a = expression, then a = k/expression.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Isolate 3/a first, then flip. 3/a = (bc−4)/b → a = 3b/(bc−4).

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — requires careful fraction work but no calculator.

## Question 5 🟢
**Correct Answer: B) (P − 2l)/2**

**Step-by-step:**
P = 2l + 2w. Subtract 2l: P − 2l = 2w. Divide by 2: w = (P − 2l)/2.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- A) Forgot to divide by 2.
- C) (P − l)/2 — subtracted l instead of 2l before dividing.
- D) P/2 − l/2 — distributed the division incorrectly.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: C is the most common error — students subtract only one l instead of 2l. D splits the fraction incorrectly.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Subtract 2l first (not l), then divide the entire remaining expression by 2.

⚡ FAST METHOD: P − 2l = 2w → w = (P − 2l)/2.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — standard formula rearrangement.

## Question 6 🟡

2(x − 3y) = 7y + 8.
Distribute: 2x − 6y = 7y + 8.
Add 6y: 2x = 13y + 8.
Divide by 2: x = (13y + 8)/2.

Note: C says x = 13y/2 + 4 and D says x = (7y + 8 + 6y)/2, both of which simplify to the same expression as A. On the DSAT, only one form would appear alongside genuinely different wrong answers. A is the intended answer.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- B) (y + 8)/2 — forgot to add 6y when distributing (combined incorrectly).

🪤 TRAP ALERT: Forgetting to distribute the 2 on the left side (leaving x − 3y instead of 2x − 6y).

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Distribute first, then collect terms with y on one side.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Distribute, combine y-terms, divide by 2.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — standard distribution and rearrangement.

**Step-by-step:**

This means the coefficients of x must be equal and the constants must be equal.
Coefficients of x: a = b.
Constants: 5 = c.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: Students might try to solve for x, but "true for all x" means this is an identity, not an equation to solve.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: "True for all values of x" means corresponding coefficients must be equal.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Coefficients match → a = b = 3.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — coefficient matching, no computation.

## Question 8 🟡
**Correct Answer: A) C = (F − 32) × 5/9**

**Step-by-step:**
F = (9/5)C + 32.
Subtract 32: F − 32 = (9/5)C.
Multiply by 5/9: C = (F − 32)(5/9).

**Why other options are wrong:**
- B) Added 32 and multiplied by 9/5 (both operations reversed).
- C) Applied 5/9 to F but subtracted 32 after (order of operations wrong).
- D) Multiplied by 9/5 instead of 5/9.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: D uses 9/5 (the reciprocal of the correct multiplier). Students who don't flip the fraction when solving get this.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: To undo multiplication by 9/5, multiply by its reciprocal, 5/9. And subtract 32 first.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Subtract 32, then multiply by 5/9.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — well-known formula; two-step reversal.

## Question 9 🔴
**Correct Answer: A) p = q/(rq + 5)**

**Step-by-step:**
1/p − 5/q = r.
Isolate 1/p: 1/p = r + 5/q = (rq + 5)/q.
Take reciprocal: p = q/(rq + 5).

**Why other options are wrong:**
- B) This is 1/p, not p (forgot to take the reciprocal).
- C) Used r − 5/q instead of r + 5/q (wrong sign when moving 5/q).
- D) This is equivalent to A when simplified (1/(r + 5/q) = q/(rq + 5)). On the DSAT, only the simplified form would appear.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: B is the most dangerous trap — it's 1/p, not p. When the target variable is in a denominator, you must take the reciprocal at the end.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Isolate 1/p first, combine fractions, then flip.

⚡ FAST METHOD: 1/p = (rq + 5)/q → p = q/(rq + 5).

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — fraction algebra with reciprocals.

## Question 12 🔴
**Correct Answer: A) x = (z + 1)(y − 3)/2**

**Step-by-step:**
2x/(y − 3) = z + 1.
Multiply both sides by (y − 3): 2x = (z + 1)(y − 3).
Divide by 2: x = (z + 1)(y − 3)/2.

Note: C and D attempt to expand (z + 1)(y − 3):
(z + 1)(y − 3) = zy − 3z + y − 3.
So x = (zy − 3z + y − 3)/2, which is C. C is equivalent to A (just expanded).

**Why other options are wrong:**
- B) 2(z+1)/(y−3) — multiplied by (y−3) incorrectly (moved it to the denominator instead of numerator).
- D) (zy + y − 3z − 3)/2 — same as C (reordered terms), both equivalent to A. On a real test, only one form appears.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: B divides by (y − 3) instead of multiplying. When a fraction equals something, multiply both sides by the denominator.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Multiply both sides by the denominator to clear the fraction, then divide by the remaining coefficient.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Cross-multiply: 2x = (z+1)(y−3). Then x = (z+1)(y−3)/2.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — cross multiplication, then divide.

## Question 15 🔴
**Correct Answer: A) t = s − (3s + 2)/r**

**Step-by-step:**
r = (3s + 2)/(s − t).
Multiply both sides by (s − t): r(s − t) = 3s + 2.
Distribute: rs − rt = 3s + 2.
Isolate t: −rt = 3s + 2 − rs → rt = rs − 3s − 2 → t = (rs − 3s − 2)/r.

Alternatively: t = s − (3s + 2)/r (which is the same thing):
t = rs/r − (3s + 2)/r = (rs − 3s − 2)/r. ✓

Note: C says (rs − 3s − 2)/r, which equals A when simplified. D says s(r−3)/r − 2/r = (sr − 3s − 2)/r, which also equals A. All three (A, C, D) are equivalent.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- B) (3s + 2 − rs)/r — this is −t, not t (sign error).

🪤 TRAP ALERT: B has the sign reversed. When dividing −rt = 3s + 2 − rs by −r, every term changes sign.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Careful sign management when isolating a subtracted variable. Double-check by substituting back.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Cross-multiply, distribute, isolate t-term, divide by its coefficient.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — algebraic manipulation with careful signs.

## Question 19 🔴
**Correct Answer: A) k = (2n − 6 + 2j)/(j − n + 3)**

**Step-by-step:**
j/(k + 2) = (n − 3)/(k − 2).
Cross-multiply: j(k − 2) = (n − 3)(k + 2).
Expand: jk − 2j = nk + 2n − 3k − 6.
Collect k-terms on the left: jk − nk + 3k = 2j + 2n − 6.
Factor: k(j − n + 3) = 2j + 2n − 6.
Divide: k = (2j + 2n − 6)/(j − n + 3) = (2n − 6 + 2j)/(j − n + 3).

**Why other options are wrong:**
- B) Denominator is (n − 3 − j) = −(j − n + 3), giving the negative of the correct answer.
- C) Denominator is (j + n − 3), which has the wrong sign on n (should be j − n + 3).
- D) Numerator is negated — (−2j − 2n + 6) = −(2j + 2n − 6).

🪤 TRAP ALERT: Sign errors when collecting k-terms. Moving nk and −3k across the equals sign requires changing their signs.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Cross-multiply, expand, collect all k-terms on one side, factor out k.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Cross-multiply → expand → collect k-terms → factor → divide.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — multi-step algebra; careful sign tracking is essential.

## Question 20 🟡
**Correct Answer: D) x = 4(y − 2)/5 − 3**

**Step-by-step:**
(x + 3)/4 = (y − 2)/5.
Multiply both sides by 4: x + 3 = 4(y − 2)/5.
Subtract 3: x = 4(y − 2)/5 − 3.

This can also be written as x = (4y − 8)/5 − 3 = (4y − 8 − 15)/5 = (4y − 23)/5.
So A is x = (4y − 8)/5 − 3 = (4y − 8 − 15)/5 = (4y − 23)/5, and B is (4y − 23)/5.

Checking: A = (4y − 8)/5 − 3. This equals (4y − 8)/5 − 15/5 = (4y − 23)/5 = B.
And D = 4(y − 2)/5 − 3 = (4y − 8)/5 − 3 = A = B.

So A, B, and D are all equivalent! On a real DSAT, only one form would appear.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- C) Contains x on both sides — this is not solved for x.

🪤 TRAP ALERT: C is a circular equation (x appears on both sides). It doesn't isolate x.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Multiply both sides by the denominator of the fraction containing x, then subtract to isolate.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Multiply by 4, subtract 3.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — cross-multiplication approach.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- B) (5u − 5v − 3)/2 — this is 1/w × (u−v), not w.
- C) Correct algebraic form but not simplified into a single fraction.
- D) Same as A: 5(u−v) − 3 = 5u − 5v − 3. So D is equivalent to A. ✓

🪤 TRAP ALERT: Forgetting to take the reciprocal at the end gives 1/w instead of w.

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Isolate the fraction containing w, combine the right side, then take the reciprocal.

⚡ FAST METHOD: Isolate 2/w → combine right side → flip.

📱✍️ VERDICT: Hand-solve — multi-step fraction manipulation.

## 📊 Topic Summary — Literal Equations

**Core Concepts Tested:**
1. Isolating a variable in a multi-variable equation
2. Handling fractions with variables in numerator and denominator
3. Recognizing identities ("true for all values of x")
4. Rearranging standard formulas (area, perimeter, volume, interest, temperature)
5. Cross-multiplication and reciprocal operations
6. Order of operations when solving (subtract before dividing, etc.)

**Most Common Traps:**
- Getting 1/x instead of x (forgetting to take the reciprocal)
- Sign errors when moving terms across the equals sign
- Parentheses errors: (c − 12)/7 ≠ c − 12/7
- Dividing by the wrong quantity (coefficient confusion)
- Confusing "true for all x" with "solve for x"

**Desmos vs. Hand-Solve Breakdown:**
- Hand-solve: 25 out of 25 questions (100%) — literal equations are purely algebraic manipulation
- Desmos: 0 out of 25 questions (0%) — these are symbolic problems; Desmos can't rearrange formulas
