Q1. Answer: A) detect
Why? The passage talks about realistic pieces where the viewer can easily see familiar objects. 'Detect' means to
notice or spot something — it fits perfectly. 'Recall' means to remember (wrong idea). 'Dismiss' means to ignore
(opposite). 'Classify' means to sort into groups (not about seeing).
Q2. Answer: B) appreciation of
Why? Critics helped people value radio by comparing it to concerts. 'Appreciation of' means having a positive view of
something. 'Contribution to' doesn't make sense here. 'Imitation of' and 'dependence on' miss the point about building
respect for the medium.
Q3. Answer: A) meticulousness
Why? The illustrators checked living plants, dried samples, AND expert scientists to make sure every detail was right.
That is extreme care = meticulousness. 'Obscurity' means being unknown. 'Brevity' means shortness. 'Spontaneity'
means being unplanned — all wrong.
Q4. Answer: A) settle in
Why? The sentence describes companies grouping together in one area. 'Settle in' means to set up in a place. 'Defer
to' means to give in to someone. 'Compete with' and 'agree on' don't describe moving to a location.
Q5. Answer: C) familiar to
Why? The last sentence is the clue: fancy stage setups 'were not common until the 1800s.' So before that, simple
stages were normal — audiences were used to them. 'Familiar to' fits. Simple stages wouldn't be 'puzzling' or 'thrilling' if
they were the everyday standard.
Q6. Answer: A) undermine
Why? Bad data from inexperienced volunteers weakens the survey results. 'Undermine' means to damage or weaken.
'Confirm' is the opposite. 'Advertise' and 'predict' don't relate to weakening reliability.
Q7. Answer: D
Why? First the text says people think highly of Mr. Hargrove. Then it lists the things he DOES to keep that good image
(stays quiet, hides his thoughts, never gives people a reason to doubt him). So: good reputation first, then behaviors
that maintain it. That is choice D.
Q8. Answer: D
Why? The text describes a phenomenon (parallel adaptation), then mentions two studies that each looked at it
differently (Ishida on shared pathways, Kokko on distinct pathways), then mentions a third study (Ramanathan and
Delgado combining both). That matches D: phenomenon → two studies → third study.
Q9. Answer: B
Why? The narrator says she was 'never before so conscious of standing at a turning point.' She feels this specific
moment is a big life change. That is B. She's not excited about money (A), she doesn't have a plan (C), and she doesn't
mention thanking people (D).
Q10. Answer: A
Why? The energy staircase says: when people earn more money, they switch to cleaner fuels. So a supporter would
explain India's change simply: incomes rose → families could afford cleaner fuel. That is A. Choice B talks about
subsidies (a different explanation). C talks about biomass getting expensive (not the staircase model). D says the link

Q11. Answer: A) 58,000 metric tons of tin in 2022
