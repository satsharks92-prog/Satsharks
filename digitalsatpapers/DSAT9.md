**Question 22**
*Skill: Triangle Similarity — Sufficient Conditions*

For triangle ABC and triangle DEF, angles A and D each measure 40°, BC = 18 and EF = 12. Which additional piece(s) of information is(are) sufficient to prove that triangle ABC is similar to triangle DEF?

I. The length of AB is (3/2) the length of DE.
II. The length of AC is (3/2) the length of DF.

A) I is sufficient, but II is not.
B) II is sufficient, but I is not.
C) I is sufficient, and II is sufficient.
D) Neither I nor II is sufficient.

**Answer: D**

*(Explanation: We know ∠A = ∠D = 40° and BC/EF = 18/12 = 3/2. BC and EF are opposite the known equal angles, not sides that include the angles. For SAS similarity, we need two sides that include the known angle to be proportional. Statement I gives AB/DE = 3/2 (one side forming the angle), and Statement II gives AC/DF = 3/2 (the other side forming the angle). Either one alone gives us AA? No—we only have one pair of equal angles. With one pair of equal angles plus one pair of proportional sides adjacent to that angle, we cannot conclude similarity. We would need both I and II together (SAS), or another angle. So neither alone is sufficient.)*
