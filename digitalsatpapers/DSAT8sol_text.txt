Question 18 (Geometry — Angles)
Answer: A) (−14x + 280)°
When two lines intersect, they form two pairs of vertical angles. If one angle is (7x − 140)°, its vertical angle
is also (7x − 140)°, and the supplementary angles are each (180 − (7x − 140))° = (320 − 7x)°. Possible sums:
two acute vertical angles = 2(7x − 140) = (14x − 280)°; two obtuse vertical angles = 2(320 − 7x) = (−14x +
640)°; two adjacent angles = 180°. The sum (−14x + 280)° does not correspond to any valid pair of angles,
so it could NOT be achieved.
